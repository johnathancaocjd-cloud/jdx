US Stocks Rise as Investors Weigh Economic Data and Earnings

A wider read of yesterday's session — sector rotation, pre-market action, watchlist scan, and what to watch into the US open at 21:30 HKT.

TL;DR

The US stock market rose on Friday, with the S&P 500 gaining +0.37% to close at 7,473.47. The Nasdaq 100 outperformed, rising +0.42% to 29,481.64. Beneath the surface, sector rotation was notable, with XLV and XLI leading the charge.

The market's gains were driven by a combination of factors, including a decline in the 10Y Yield to 4.56 and a rally in Gold to 4,538.50. However, the VIX fell -0.66% to 16.59, indicating a decrease in market volatility.

What Could Break the Tape

BULLISH TRIGGERS
  • A strong earnings report from a major technology company, such as AAPL or GOOGL.
  • A positive surprise in economic data, such as a higher-than-expected GDP growth rate.
  • A breakthrough in trade negotiations between the US and China, leading to a reduction in tariffs and increased trade activity.
BEARISH TRIGGERS
  • A disappointing earnings report from a major company, such as MSFT or JPM.
  • A negative surprise in economic data, such as a lower-than-expected jobs growth rate.
  • An escalation in tensions between the US and China, leading to increased tariffs and decreased trade activity.
